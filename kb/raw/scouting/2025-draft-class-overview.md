## Key Takeaways
- The 2025 draft class is considered strong at the top, with clear tiers
- Cooper Flagg (Duke) is the consensus #1 — 6'9" two-way forward, elite motor and IQ
- Dylan Harper (Rutgers) and Ace Bailey (Rutgers) are the next tier — both projected top 5
- VJ Edgecombe (Baylor) is a dynamic athlete with upside, potential top-5 pick
- The Nets could have multiple picks in this draft via their own pick + Rockets pick (top-4 protected) + Suns 2025 + Knicks 2025 (though Knicks pick likely late)

## Notes
### Tier 1 — Franchise Player
**Cooper Flagg** (Duke, 6'9" F) — The clear #1. Two-way dominance, plays like a 10-year vet at 18. Comparison: prime Jayson Tatum with better defense. Every team wants him.

### Tier 2 — All-Star Upside
**Dylan Harper** (Rutgers, 6'6" G) — Smooth scoring guard, elite shot creation. Jersey kid, would be a hometown story in Brooklyn. Comparison: Brandon Roy.
**Ace Bailey** (Rutgers, 6'9" F) — Long, fluid wing with deep range. Shot creation at his size is rare. Comparison: young Tracy McGrady.

### Tier 3 — High Upside
**VJ Edgecombe** (Baylor, 6'5" G) — Explosive athlete, transition terror. Needs polish but the tools are elite. Comparison: Jalen Green with better defense.
**Kasparas Jakucionis** (Illinois, 6'5" G) — Crafty playmaker with size. International IQ. Comparison: Manu Ginobili lite.

## Nets Draft Position Context
The Nets are tanking for the best possible pick. Their own pick is the most valuable asset in this scenario — a top-3 pick in this class could be the centerpiece of the rebuild.
