## Key Takeaways
- Beat consensus is that the Nets are on a 3-4 year rebuild timeline (competitive by 2027-28)
- The pick haul is viewed as historically strong — possibly the best asset chest any rebuilding team has ever had
- Sean Marks has earned trust from ownership to execute a patient rebuild
- The 2025 draft is viewed as the most critical inflection point

## Notes
Multiple beat reporters (Fischer, Windhorst, Bondy) have noted that the Nets' rebuild is unusual because of how many high-value picks they hold. Most rebuilding teams have their own picks plus maybe one extra first. The Nets have 7+ first-round picks over the next several drafts, many unprotected from teams (Suns, Knicks) that could decline.

The challenge is converting picks to players. The Nets need to nail their lottery picks and develop young talent — the front office's track record on development is unproven since they've been in "win now" mode since 2019.

## Quotes
> "The Nets have the best war chest in the NBA. The question isn't assets — it's whether they can develop." — Bobby Marks, ESPN

> "Brooklyn is in year one of what should be a very patient rebuild. They have the picks to make it work." — Jake Fischer, Yahoo Sports
