## Key Takeaways
- Nets traded Mikal Bridges to the Knicks on June 26, 2024
- Return: Bojan Bogdanovic, four unprotected first-round picks (2025, 2027, 2029, 2031), a 2028 pick swap, and Shake Milton
- Combined with the KD/Kyrie returns, the Nets now hold a historic haul of future first-round picks
- This was the pivot point from "competitive rebuild" to full asset accumulation

## Notes
The Bridges trade was controversial among fans who had grown attached to Mikal as the face of the post-superstar era. But Sean Marks and the front office correctly identified that a Bridges-led team was a play-in ceiling, and the Knicks' desperation to pair him with Villanova teammates Brunson and Hart created a seller's market.

The unprotected nature of all four picks is critical — the Knicks are betting their entire future on the Brunson core staying healthy and competitive through 2031. Any decline makes these picks extremely valuable.

Pick haul after this trade:
- Suns 2025, 2027, 2029 (unprotected) + 2028 swap
- Knicks 2025, 2027, 2029, 2031 (unprotected) + 2028 swap
- Rockets 2025 (from Harden trade, top-4 protected)
- Mavs 2025, 2027 (from Kyrie trade)
