## Key Takeaways
- The Nets used FIVE first-round picks in the 2025 draft — the most by any team in a single draft in modern NBA history
- Cooper Flagg went #1 overall to the Dallas Mavericks, not the Nets
- The Nets picked #8 overall (their own pick), missing the top tier

## Nets Draft Picks

### Round 1
- **Pick 8**: Egor Demin (PG, 6'8", BYU) — Russian point guard with elite size and vision. The centerpiece pick.
- **Pick 19**: Nolan Traore (PG, 6'3", Saint-Quentin, France) — French speedster, explosive athlete
- **Pick 22**: Drake Powell (SG, 6'5", North Carolina) — Two-way wing, defensive specialist
- **Pick 26**: Ben Saraf (SG, 6'6", Israel/Ratiopharm Ulm) — Crafty international guard with scoring touch
- **Pick 27**: Danny Wolf (PF, 6'11", Michigan) — Stretch big with passing ability, Israeli national

### Round 2
- **Pick 36**: Adou Thiero (SF, Arkansas) — Athletic wing, developmental prospect

## Pick Sources
- Pick 8: Nets own pick (lottery — landed #8 despite tanking for top 3)
- Pick 19: Via Phoenix Suns (from KD trade)
- Pick 22: Via trade chain (originally Atlanta pick, routed to Brooklyn)
- Pick 26: Via New York Knicks (from Bridges trade)
- Pick 27: Via Houston Rockets (from Harden trade — top-4 protection lifted, conveyed at #27)

## Notes
The Nets landed at #8 in the lottery despite having a bottom-3 record — a brutal lottery outcome that cost them a shot at Flagg, Harper, or Bailey. However, Egor Demin at #8 is seen as a potential franchise point guard given his 6'8" frame and playmaking ability.

The Rockets pick (#27) finally conveyed after being top-4 protected — Houston finished with a good enough record that the protection didn't kick in.

Five first-rounders in one draft is an unprecedented investment in youth. The Nets essentially drafted an entire starting lineup.
