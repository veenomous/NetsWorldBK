## Key Takeaways
- On July 8, 2025, the Nets traded Cameron Johnson to the Denver Nuggets for Michael Porter Jr. and a 2032 first-round pick
- This was part of a historic seven-team trade on July 6-8, 2025
- The Nets received MPJ (24.2 PPG scorer) plus another first-round pick, extending their asset haul
- Cameron Johnson — the last remaining player from the original KD trade return — was moved for a younger, higher-ceiling player plus a pick
- MPJ's contract is larger but he's 27 and a proven 20+ PPG scorer

## Notes
The Cam Johnson era in Brooklyn is over. He was the emotional anchor of the post-KD rebuild and the "keep or trade" debate defined much of the 2024-25 season discourse. The front office chose "trade" — getting a 2032 first (extending the pick haul even further) and MPJ, who immediately became the team's best player.

MPJ's fit is interesting: he's a legitimate scorer who can anchor the offense while the rookies develop. He's not a long-term rebuild piece (he'll be 30+ when the Nets compete) but he gives the young players a real NBA offense to learn in.
