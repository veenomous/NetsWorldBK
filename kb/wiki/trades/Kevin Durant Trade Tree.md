## Summary
The Kevin Durant trade to the Phoenix Suns on February 9, 2023, is the foundational transaction of the Brooklyn Nets rebuild — and its branches now extend further than anyone imagined. The original return (Mikal Bridges, Cam Johnson, 4 unprotected Suns firsts) has since cascaded: Bridges was flipped to the Knicks for 4 more firsts, and Cam Johnson was traded to Denver for Michael Porter Jr. and a 2032 first. The KD trade tree has produced 9+ first-round picks, 2 swaps, a franchise scorer in MPJ, and five rookies drafted in 2025. It may be the most consequential trade in NBA history.

## Key Insights
- Original return: Bridges + Cam Johnson + 4 unprotected Suns firsts + 2028 swap
- Bridges flipped to Knicks (June 2024): 4 unprotected Knicks firsts + 2028 swap
- Cam Johnson flipped to Denver (July 2025): [[Michael Porter Jr.]] + 2032 Denver first
- Five of the Nets' 2025 draft picks (Demin, Traore, Powell, Saraf, Wolf) trace back to the KD trade chain
- Total first-round picks traceable to the KD trade: 9 (4 Suns + 4 Knicks + 1 Denver 2032)
- The trade tree is still growing — Suns picks extend to 2029, Knicks to 2031, Denver to 2032
