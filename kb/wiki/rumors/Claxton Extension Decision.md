## Summary
[[Nic Claxton]] is locked in through 2027-28 on his $100M deal, but the Nets face a strategic decision: is he the long-term center, or should they explore trading him while his value is high? At 26, Claxton's prime aligns with the rebuild timeline — but his lack of shooting limits offensive ceiling.

## What We Know
- Claxton has 2 years left on his 4-year, $100M contract (through 2027-28)
- He's the Nets' best defender and most experienced player alongside MPJ
- His .186 career 3PT% is a liability in modern NBA spacing
- He averaged 10.3 PPG, 7.4 RPG in 2025-26 — solid but not All-Star level
- Danny Wolf (6'11" stretch big) could eventually take his starting spot if Claxton is traded

## Scenarios
1. **Keep Claxton**: He anchors the defense through the competitive window. Pair him with a stretch 4 (Clowney or Wolf).
2. **Trade Claxton**: His $25.4M salary + defensive reputation has value to contenders. Return could be a first-round pick + young player.
3. **Extend Claxton**: Only if the Nets believe he's a championship-level center. Risky given his offensive limitations.

## Confidence Level
**Medium** — No trade rumors have surfaced. This is a strategic question the front office is certainly evaluating.
