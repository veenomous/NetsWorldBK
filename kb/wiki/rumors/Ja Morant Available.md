## Summary
Memphis is tearing it down. Jaren Jackson Jr. and Desmond Bane are gone. And now ESPN reports that the Grizzlies are actively shopping Ja Morant. His trade value is reportedly low — Sacramento asked Memphis to *add picks* to sweeten a deal — which means a team with the right combination of young talent and draft capital could steal a former All-Star point guard. Brooklyn fits that description exactly.

## What We Know
- ESPN (April 2026): Memphis GM Zach Kleiman confirmed the rebuild is about "organizational direction," not any one player
- Grizzlies already traded Jaren Jackson Jr. and Desmond Bane — full teardown mode
- Sacramento Kings discussed a Morant deal but asked Memphis to include draft picks — signaling weak trade market
- The Trae Young precedent: Young went to Washington for CJ McCollum and Corey Kispert with zero draft picks attached
- One scout told ESPN "there's going to be hesitation" but "several desperate teams" may pursue Morant
- **The Nets were NOT mentioned in the article** — this is speculative from our end

## Why Brooklyn Could Be a Fit
The Nets have what Memphis would want in a rebuild package:
- **Young players on rookie deals**: [[Noah Clowney]], [[Nolan Traore]], [[Drake Powell]], [[Danny Wolf]]
- **Future first-round picks**: Knicks 2027, 2029, 2031 + Dallas 2027
- **Salary matching**: [[Michael Porter Jr.]] at $38.3M could be the matching salary piece

A hypothetical package: MPJ (salary match) + Clowney or Traore (young player) + 1-2 future firsts. That's competitive with what other teams can offer, especially if Morant's market is as weak as reported.

## Why It Might Not Make Sense
- **Morant's off-court issues**: Suspensions, the gun incidents, maturity concerns. Brooklyn just exited the Kyrie drama — do they want another high-maintenance star?
- **Timeline**: Morant is 26. The Nets project competitive in 2027-28. But acquiring him now means paying a max contract through the development years.
- **The Demin question**: If [[Egor Demin]] is the franchise point guard, adding Morant creates a logjam. You don't trade for a star at the same position as your franchise bet.
- **Cost vs. value**: If Morant's market is truly weak (Sacramento asked Memphis to ADD picks), the Nets might wait and get him cheaper later.

## The Real Question
Would [[Sean Marks Era|Sean Marks]] shift from "patient rebuild" to "accelerate with a star" for Ja Morant? The [NY Post reported](https://nypost.com) that Marks is keeping the timeline "murky" — is this why? Or is Brooklyn's path staying the course with Demin and the draft picks?
