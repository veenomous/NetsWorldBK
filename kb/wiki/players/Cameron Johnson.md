## Summary
Cameron Johnson is the most important player on the Nets roster — the last active return from the Kevin Durant trade and the bridge between the rebuild's accumulation phase and its competitive phase. A 6'8" wing who shoots .415 from three on high volume, he represents either the Nets' best building block or their most valuable trade chip.

## Key Insights
- Acquired from Phoenix in the KD trade (February 2023) along with Mikal Bridges and four first-round picks
- Signed a 4-year, $94M extension with the Nets — shows mutual commitment
- Averaged 19.4 PPG on 41.5% 3PT shooting in 2023-24 — elite spacing at the 4
- His shooting, size (6'8"), and defensive versatility make him a fit on any roster
- At 28 (as of 2024-25), he's older than the typical rebuild centerpiece — the age-timeline question is real
- Missed significant time with injuries (meniscus, ankle) — durability is a concern

## Details

### Playing Style
Cam is a 3-and-D forward with scoring chops. He spaces the floor at an elite level, can operate as a secondary creator in the midrange, and defends 2-4. His shooting gravity opens driving lanes for guards — making him valuable even when his shot isn't falling.

### Contract Situation
4 years, $94M (signed 2023). Team-friendly relative to his production. This contract makes him both a keepable cornerstone and a tradeable asset — any contender would absorb this deal.

### The Keep vs. Trade Debate
**Case for keeping:** Elite shooter, good culture guy, signed long-term. You need veterans to develop young players. His shooting complements any lottery pick guard/forward.
**Case for trading:** He'll be 30+ by the time the Nets are competitive. His value is highest now. Could yield another first-round pick from a contender.

### Injury History
- 2022-23: Meniscus surgery (missed 2+ months)
- 2023-24: Ankle sprains (missed multiple games)
- Durability is the biggest knock — when healthy, he's a legitimate starter on a playoff team
