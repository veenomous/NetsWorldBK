Architecture

Differentiation
What makes
this special
  • 1

    "Pull the Thread" UX

    See a player name anywhere on the site. Click it. Trace every connection: who they were traded for, what picks came back, who was drafted with those picks, how those players are performing. Every entity is a portal into the knowledge graph.

  • 2

    KB-Powered Tools

    The Trade Machine sidebar shows the player's wiki article. The War Room shows pick inventory context. The Lottery Sim links to Tank Math. The KB isn't a section — it's the brain that powers every tool on the site.

  • 3

    Visual Knowledge Graph

    Force-directed graph explorer where fans click through a visual web of players, trades, picks, and seasons. Nobody in sports has shipped this publicly. It's the "wow" feature.

  • 4

    Confidence + Freshness

    Every fact shows when it was last verified and where it came from. "NBA.com official, high confidence" vs. "beat reporter tweet, medium confidence." Builds trust that no wiki has.

  • 5

    Cap Narrative Layer

    Spotrac tells you the numbers. The Nets Wiki tells you the story. How did the Nets get here? Which trades caused it? What's the path out? Data with meaning.

  • 6

    Draft Outcome Tracker

    Every Nets draft pick since 2012: where they are now, how they panned out, who was picked after them. A "were we right?" scoreboard. Nobody does this well.

Scalability
Built for
every team

The architecture is team-agnostic. Prove it with the Nets, then replicate for all 30 teams. The data pipeline, templates, and tools are shared infrastructure.

🔄

Universal Structure

raw/ and wiki/ categories (players, trades, seasons, draft) apply to every NBA team. Swap the seed content and the KB works for any franchise.

🤖

Shared Agent

The data pipeline (ESPN, BBRef, Spotrac) serves all 30 teams. One agent with a team filter parameter populates any team's KB automatically.

🎨

Per-Team Voice

The facts are universal. The editorial voice is team-specific. Each fanbase has its own culture, tone, and concerns. The agent compiles facts; the community adds personality.

💰

Platform Play

White-label ("YourTeamGrit") or multi-tenant with team namespaces. Infrastructure is shared. Content is per-team. Network effects grow with each team added.

30 teams × millions of fans per team = massive TAM

Build it for BKGrit. If fans use it, you have a platform. If they don't, you learned cheap.
